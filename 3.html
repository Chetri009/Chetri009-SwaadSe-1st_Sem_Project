Roti Canai

Roti Canai is a crispy and flaky Malaysian flatbread, often served with a side of curry or dhal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups All-Purpose Flour
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 1 tbsp Sugar
  • 1/2 cup Warm Water
  • 1/4 cup Condensed Milk
  • 2 tbsp Ghee or Melted Butter (plus extra for frying)
  • Vegetable Oil (for kneading and soaking)

Steps:

  1. In a large bowl, mix flour, salt, and sugar.
  2. Combine warm water, condensed milk, and ghee in a separate bowl.
  3. Gradually add the wet mixture to the dry ingredients and knead until a smooth, elastic dough forms.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls (about 8 pieces) and coat them lightly with oil.
  5. Place the dough balls in a tray, cover with plastic wrap, and let them rest for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  6. On a greased surface, flatten each dough ball with your hands and stretch it into a thin, almost transparent sheet.
  7. Fold the edges of the sheet inward to form a rectangular or circular shape.
  8. Heat a griddle or non-stick pan over medium heat. Add a small amount of ghee and cook each roti until golden brown and flaky, flipping once.
  9. Serve hot with curry, dhal, or sugar for dipping.
